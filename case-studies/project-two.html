Case Study · News Writing · CMI 3315 · News 3

Young Adults Worry About Guns, Push for Reform

Type Reported Feature
Course CMI 3315 — Intro to Web Design
Date April 14, 2026
Sources 4 interviews · Everytown · CDC

Project Overview

This reported feature examines how young adults especially college students are responding to the persistent reality of gun violence on campuses. The article draws on interviews with students affected by campus shootings, advocacy voices, and national data from Everytown Research, the K-12 School Shooting Database, and the CDC.

The story was written as News Story 3 and represents the most research intensive article I've completed in the journalism program so far.

The Challenge

Writing about gun violence responsibly requires balancing two tensions: honoring the real experiences of people who have been directly affected, and avoiding sensationalism that could distort the story. The challenge was to root the piece in data and expert voices while centering the human experience, particularly that of students who now navigate campus life with a different relationship to safety than students a generation before them.

I also had to make sure that the story covered multiple perspectives fairly, including policy advocates, student voices from campuses, and local Texas Tech student reactions to national events.

My Role

I was the sole reporter and writer. I identified sources, conducted all interviews, gathered and verified data from multiple organizations.

Process & Research

I began by identifying the news hook of the Florida State University shooting in April 2025 and then broadened outward to the national trend. I pulled data from:

  • Everytown Research: 400+ gunfire incidents on college campuses since 2013
  • K-12 School Shooting Database: 4,400+ children and teens killed annually
  • CDC death certificate data: Firearms remain a leading cause of death for ages 18–24
  • American Communities Project: Violence in college towns and student safety

I then sought interviews that could ground those statistics in lived experience. Madelyne Soto, a Florida State University student who was on campus during the April 2025 shooting, provided the emotional anchor of the piece. Erik Frantz, a Texas Tech junior, brought the local angle. Clarissa Morales, an activist and advocate, addressed the prevention and policy dimension.

Key Quotes & Sources

The article centers several quotes that capture the emotional and civic response to gun violence on campuses. Soto described the moment she realized she was in danger: the sight of abandoned belongings made the event feel real and deeply unsettling. Frantz reflected on how national incidents affect how students at all campuses think about safety even when shootings occur far away. Morales framed her advocacy work as pushing for preventative measures so students can focus on their education.

Solution & Final Article

The final piece is a multisource reported feature that moves from a scene setting lead an untouched backpack near a campus bench through national data and firsthand interviews, to a conclusion about the ongoing policy debate. The revised version tightened the transitions, strengthened the data attribution, and improved sentence variety throughout.

Reflection

This story pushed me to think carefully about the ethics of trauma reporting. When someone shares their experience of being in a mass shooting, you carry a real responsibility to get it right not just factually, but tonally. I learned to ask open questions and then listen, rather than leading a source toward the angle I already had in mind. The revision process also taught me that a strong lead and a clearly stated nut graph make every subsequent paragraph easier to write.
